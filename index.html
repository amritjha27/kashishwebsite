PUBLISHED RESEARCH

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on the education system globally, resulting in the shift towards online learning for students. While this mode of instruction has proved successful, studies have revealed that it is also associated with various psycho-emotional, mental and physical health issues. In light of this, we conducted a study to investigate the stress levels experienced by high school students during the pandemic, as well as the relationship between sleep quality and perceived stress. A sample of 109 high school students from Chandigarh were selected for the study, and a self-designed questionnaire for sleep quality and perceived stress was administered to them via Google forms. Our findings revealed that the majority of students experienced stress in the form of academic stress (41.8%), psycho-emotional stress (54.5%), stress due to daily hassles (36.4%), and physical stress (37.5%). Additionally, we discovered that there was an inverse relationship between the sleep quality of high school students and their perceived stress levels during the COVID-19 pandemic.

NGO ALFAAZ

“Change is the only constant,” and it is in our hands to ensure that it is a positive one. NGO Alfaaz is a modern-day non governmental organization run by the passionate youth, taking on the responsibility to spread the message of sustainability and peaceful coexistence, as it is in the hands of our generation and the coming generations. The team focuses on the need of the hour, attempting to help our society advance and overcome the plethora of social issues which have seemingly become a recurring feature. This team aims to ensure that no voice goes unheard. Through our teamwork and unity we wish to put up a trustworthy and expressive environment for our community. We believe, together, we can foster a healthy environment for all those who continue to feel unsafe. Life is ephemeral, which is why we must progress, not only on a personal level but as a community. Therefore, we encourage everyone to take up the charge of making change possible, to join in as a drop of water, accompanying us as we build the mighty ocean. As Franklin D. Roosevelt once said, "We cannot always build the future for our youth, but we can build our youth for the future".

DRAMA AND THEATER

"Oscar Wilde once said that theater is the greatest of all art forms, as it allows individuals to share a sense of humanity with one another. This sentiment was brought to life at Mayo College Girls' School during a four-day Dramatics Workshop on Improvisational Theater, led by the accomplished actor, Sir Gavin Noel Methalaka. Over the course of the Level-1 Introduction Course, participants learned to master the art of portraying characters through body language, building and simplifying storylines, and utilizing the concept of "Yes And" to accept and build upon given situations. The workshop not only helped participants develop their skills as actors, but also gave them the tools to navigate and build upon unexpected challenges on stage and in real life.".

ROBOTICS AND AUTOMATION

I was delighted to be chosen to take part in the Robotics Workshop at the Robotics Lab in Mayo College Girls' School from 7th to 8th September, 2022. We were privileged to learn from Mr. Sanjay Gupta, an IIT Delhi graduate, who briefed us on the fundamentals of Arduino Technology. Then, with the help of LED lights, Motors, Water Pumps, Temperature Detectors, Moisture Sensors, LDRs and more, we built projects. The best parts of this experience were the insightful conversations on the basics of Robotics and the practical experience with the Arduino kits which further solidified my concepts.
